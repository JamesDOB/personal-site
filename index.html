001 — About

I make things work
for real people.

Not in a theoretical, deck-slide kind of way. I mean the kind of work where you talk to users until you actually understand what's broken, build the thing, watch it land, and iterate until it's right.

My background cuts across community building, strategy, marketing, customer success, and sales — building systems and mechanisms that meet people where they are, to help them get where they want to be.

Before I got into tech, I was an ELA teacher in Brooklyn — at the very school I attended as a kid. That experience shaped everything. It's why I care so deeply about EdTech, CivicTech, the social safety net, and adult learning. I know firsthand what it means when those systems work, and what it costs when they don't.

Right now I'm deep in AI-enabled approaches to learning — not the hype, the practical stuff. Can AI analyze a tutoring session and give real coaching feedback? Yes. I built it. And in a world where everyone can build, taste is king.
